      Register a birth

      In Portugal, when a child is born, their registry is mandatory. Therefore, it is necessary to declare their birth.

      All children born in Portugal must be registered, even if the parents are foreign.

      WARNING

      Service at the Born Citizen counters has been suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic, but is gradually being reopen. Reopenings are being communicated through the Justice portal.

      It is possible to register a birth online or in person, in a registry office, but in this case it will be necessary to previously schedule the service. 

      COVID-19 RESTRICTIONS

      Information about in-person services may be out of date. Given the situation caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, it is necessary to schedule for most public service counters.

      Who can Register a birth?

      • Any or both parents
      • Any legal representative of the parents
      • Any close relation with knowledge of the birth
      • A director or administrator, or any designated employee, of the health unit where the child was born, or where the birth was declared.

      Where can you Register a birth?

      • Via the internet, on Declaração de Nascimento online, in the portuguese Justice portal.
      • At any Citizen Birth service desk (at the maternity or general hospitals).
      • At any civil registry office.

      What is the Price to Register a birth?

      It is free of charge.

      What is the Deadline to Register a birth?

      The birth registry must be done 20 days after the date of birth.

      How can you Register a birth?

      If you register the birth online, you must access Nascimento online and authenticate using Digital Mobile Key (Chave Móvel Digital) or Citizen Card.

      Next, whether you register online or in person, you will have to indicate:

      • the child’s name, which may have up to:
        • 2 Portuguese given names (simple or compound)
        • 4 surnames (simple or compound)
      • the child’s place of birth, which may be one of the following options:
        • the parish and the municipality of the place of birth
        • the parish and the municipality of the mother’s usual residence, in Portugal, at the date of birth.

      Before declaring the birth, consult the rules regarding:

      • the name composition
      • the given names that may be chosen
      • the maternity and paternity establishment.

      To know more, consult the webpage of the Registries and Notary Institute on births.
